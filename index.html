About Recycling

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials that can be used again.

Why is Recycling Important?

  • Reduces pollution
  • Decreases the use of raw materials
  • Saves energy

Recycling Bin Classification

Blue Bin - Paper and Cardboard

Description:The blue bin is commonly used for collecting paper products and cardboard.

Example:Newspapers, magazines, office paper, cardboard boxes, and paper packaging

Green Bin - Glass

Description:The green bin is often designated for glass items.

Example:Glass bottles, jars, and other glass containers (clear, green, or brown).

Yellow Bin - Plastics and Metals

Description: The yellow bin is typically used for plastics and metals.

Example:Plastic bottles, containers, cans, aluminum foil, and tin cans.

Brown Bin - Organic Waste

Description:The brown bin is usually for organic waste that can be composted.

Example:Food scraps, garden waste, and other biodegradable materials.

White Bin - E-Waste (Electronics)

Description:The white bin may be used for electronic waste.

Example:Old phones, computers, batteries, and other electronic devices.

Red Bin - General Waste (Non-Recyclable)

Description:The red bin is for general waste that cannot be recycled.

Example:Non-recyclable plastics, certain packaging, and sanitary waste.
